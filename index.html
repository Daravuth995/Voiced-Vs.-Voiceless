Voiceless vs Voiced Sounds

Learn the difference between sounds that vibrate your vocal cords (voiced) and sounds that don't (voiceless).

Understanding Voiced and Voiceless Sounds

In English pronunciation, consonant sounds can be either voiced or voiceless. The key difference is whether your vocal cords vibrate when you produce the sound.

Voiceless sounds are produced without vibration in the vocal cords. Examples include: /p/ as in "pat", /f/ as in "few", and /s/ as in "Sue".

Voiced sounds are produced with vibration in the vocal cords. Examples include: /b/ as in "bat", /v/ as in "view", and /z/ as in "zoo".

Try placing your hand on your throat as you say these sounds. You'll feel a vibration for voiced sounds but not for voiceless ones.

Learning Tips

Place your hand on your throat to feel the vibration for voiced sounds. This physical feedback is one of the best ways to distinguish between voiced and voiceless consonants.

Practice slowly and be patient with yourself. Mastering these distinctions will significantly improve your pronunciation and listening comprehension.
